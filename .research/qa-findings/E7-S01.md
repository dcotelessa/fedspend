# QA Findings — E7-S01
## Flagged for a follow-up story
- Fiscal-year boundary off-by-one in `buildFiscalYearOptions()`: the rule
  `now.getMonth() < 10 ? year : year + 1` classifies October (month index 9) as
  the current calendar year's FY. The US federal fiscal year rolls over on
  Oct 1, so October–December belong to the NEXT fiscal year. Correct boundary
  is `now.getMonth() < 9` (Oct/Nov/Dec → `year + 1`). Effect: during Oct–Dec
  the Fiscal Year dropdown's most-recent option is one year too low. Not fixed
  here because correcting it changes the dropdown's observable contents
  (a behavior change beyond a behavior-preserving QA pass) and it is untested.
  Fix belongs in its own story that also adds a testTable row for FY rollover.
